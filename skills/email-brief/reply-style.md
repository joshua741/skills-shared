# Reply Draft Style Guide — Email Brief Skill

## Voice Reference

All reply drafts are written using the Personal Master Prompt as the primary voice reference:
`https://www.notion.so/36692db18570815b965ef33f42bb5547`

Read the Personal Master Prompt on every run. Use it as the authoritative source on Joshua's tone, communication preferences, and language patterns. The rules below are non-negotiable minimums — the Personal Master Prompt may add further nuance.

---

## Non-Negotiable Style Rules

**Direct and plain.** No corporate language. No filler. No throat-clearing.

**Lead with the answer or the action.** Never open with context or preamble. The first sentence is the reply.

**Specific.** Always include the relevant number, date, address, or name. If it's about 4412 Birchwood, say 4412 Birchwood. If it's $287,500, say $287,500. Vague replies create follow-up emails.

**Short.** 2–4 sentences unless the situation genuinely requires more. If you find yourself writing a fifth sentence, cut two others first.

**No pleasantries.** Never open with "Hope you're doing well," "Thanks for reaching out," "Great to hear from you," or any variant. Start with the substance.

**Outcome-focused.** Every reply either closes a loop or opens the next step. If it does neither, rewrite it until it does.

**Never commit to something that contradicts an open task or a known deal constraint.** Cross-check the task list and deal intelligence before committing to a date, figure, or action in any draft.

---

## Format

Reply drafts appear inline in the briefing, indented under the email card:

```
Reply draft:
> [full draft text, ready to send]
```

Do not include From/To/Subject unless the context genuinely requires it (e.g., replying all vs. replying one). Joshua knows who he is replying to.

Do not add `[DRAFT]` or `[SUGGESTED REPLY]` headers inside the draft text. The briefing format makes it clear it's a draft.

## Examples of What Good Looks Like

**Bad:**
> Hi Mike, hope you're having a great Tuesday! Thanks so much for sending that over. I've had a chance to review everything and I think we're in good shape. I'll be following up with you soon to discuss next steps. Let me know if you need anything in the meantime!

**Good:**
> PSA is signed. Sending it back now. Title, let's target a 3-week close from today — May 27. Confirm receipt.

---

**Bad:**
> Hey, just checking in on where things stand with the offer. Let me know when you get a chance.

**Good:**
> Still at $220k cash. No seller finance on this one. If they come down to $210k I can close in 10 days. Otherwise I'm moving on.

---

## When Not to Draft a Reply

Do not generate a reply draft when:
- The email is informational only with no question or implied next step
- The email is a payment confirmation or automated notification
- Joshua has already responded (check thread history)
- A reply would require information the skill does not have access to and cannot infer

In these cases, leave the reply draft field blank or write: "No reply needed — [one-sentence reason]."
